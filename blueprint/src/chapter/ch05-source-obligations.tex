\section{The obligation boundary}

\begin{definition}[Source obligation gap]
  \label{def:source-obligation-gap}
  \uses{def:source-obligation-ledger}
  \lean{Iut.Stage1.IUTStage1SourceObligationGap}
  \leanok
  The source obligation gap is the current formal boundary between proved
  route algebra and unconstructed source mathematics.  It records the missing
  certification of the algorithmic output, the \(\SHE\)-certificate alignment,
  \qPilot{} positivity, and the normalization condition needed by the ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Gap promotion is formal]
  \label{prop:gap-promotion-formal}
  \uses{def:source-obligation-gap,def:source-obligation-ledger}
  \lean{Iut.Stage1.IUTStage1SourceObligationGap.toSourceObligations}
  \leanok
  Once the source obligation gap fields are supplied, they promote to the
  source-obligation ledger consumed by the current Stage 1 route.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  This promotion is bookkeeping.  It is valuable because it marks exactly which
  source fields remain mathematical obligations rather than burying them inside
  the final comparison theorem.
\end{proof}
