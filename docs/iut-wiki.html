Inter-universal Teichmuller theory

IUT as a map of alien arithmetic worlds.

Inter-universal Teichmuller theory is Mochizuki's proposed arithmetic deformation theory for number fields equipped with elliptic-curve data. It replaces direct comparison of ring/scheme structures by a network of Hodge theaters, theta links, log links, indeterminacies, and log-volume estimates.

Global route source objects to Diophantine bounds

Orientation

IUT is not best approached as a single calculation. It is a theory of controlled comparison between arithmetic structures that are deliberately not kept inside one global ring or scheme history. The vocabulary is unusual because the comparison method is unusual.

Objects

The starting objects are initial theta data, hyperbolic orbicurves, Frobenioid-type structures, log-shells, and Hodge theaters.

Motion

The central motion is through theta links and log links, arranged in the log-theta lattice. These links compare weakened data rather than ordinary scheme identities.

Output

The proof-critical output is a log-volume comparison in IUT III, Corollary 3.12. IUT IV then applies this comparison to Diophantine inequalities.

Wiki convention. This page explains the source theory and its formalization interfaces. It does not assert that the disputed proof is settled. When a statement is a conditional interface, the page says so.

Panoramic Route

The 2012 panoramic overview presents IUT as a response to a precise obstacle: scheme-theoretic Hodge-Arakelov theory suggests the needed height estimate, but its useful "global multiplicative subspace" picture is not generally available. IUT replaces that unavailable scheme-theoretic situation by non-scheme-theoretic deformations and then measures the resulting distortion.

Four-part route from Hodge-Arakelov motivation to Diophantine output

Hodge-Arakelov obstruction

Section 1 reviews an idealized Hodge-Arakelov theorem whose codomain contains factors weighted by q^{j^2}. If global multiplicative subspaces and canonical generators existed generally, a height bound would follow by conventional arithmetic line-bundle arguments.

Pano §1motivation

Key assignment

The overview isolates the assignment from the family {q^{j^2}} to q as the central computational problem. Since it is not a ring homomorphism inside one conventional scheme theory, IUT treats the two sides as living in distinct arithmetic holomorphic structures.

Pano §1KEY

Coricity

Coricity is the invariance of selected structures under the deformation. The overview compares it to the underlying real-analytic surface in classical Teichmuller theory: it is the part that remains usable while holomorphic structure is changed.

Pano §1core

Prime-strips as sections

Section 3 explains prime-strips as valuation-indexed sections of Spec(K) → Spec(F). This simulates the missing global multiplicative subspace and global generator data, while forcing explicit bookkeeping of arithmetic line-bundle and Frobenioid structures.

Pano §3prime-strips

Theta-link non-ring map

In the overview, the theta-link at bad places is an isomorphism of abstract monoids sending the theta-value family to the q-parameter side. Its compatibility with local Galois groups is by an unspecified abstract isomorphism, not by an identity map of one ambient ring.

Pano §3non-ring

Jacobi/Gaussian analogy

The overview relates multiradiality of the etale theta function to the invariance of the Gaussian under Fourier transform and to Jacobi's identity. The analogy marks the role of quadratic theta data and logarithmic unit geometry in the final log-volume calculation.

Pano §4theta analogy

Bogomolov-Szpiro Analogy

Mochizuki's 2016 paper compares IUT with Bogomolov's proof of the geometric Szpiro conjecture. The analogy is not a claim that the proofs are the same: it isolates a common pattern in which a precise degree computation must be combined with nontrivial estimates made in a larger geometric container.

Shared comparison pattern precise identity plus displacement/log-volume estimates

Upper-half-plane geometry

The paper formulates Bogomolov's proof through the geometry of the upper half-plane, universal coverings of SL2(R), and displacement estimates for lifted unipotent elements.

Bogomolov §1-3geometry

Degree plus estimate

The geometric proof combines a precise degree relation with nontrivial displacement estimates to bound orders of q-parameters. Mochizuki compares this with IUT's combination of a precise theta-link degree relation and multiradial log-volume estimates.

Bogomolov §3-4Szpiro

IUT similarities

The analogy identifies upper-half-plane boundary geometry with the additive symmetry part of Theta±ellNF-Hodge theaters, and compares Bogomolov's displacement containers with IUT's mono-analytic log-shell containers.

Bogomolov §4containers

Essential difference

Section 5 stresses the absence, in Bogomolov's proof, of Gaussian distributions and theta functions. These are precisely central in IUT: theta values, Galois evaluation, and processions are not optional decorations.

Bogomolov §5theta functions

Frobenius/etale analogy

Mochizuki compares holomorphic line-bundle and discriminant-modular-form data in Bogomolov's proof with Frobenius-like objects, and local systems plus fundamental groups with etale-like objects.

Bogomolov §4Frobenius/etale

Formalization use

For the wiki and Lean work, the paper is best used as an analogy for proof shape: precise correspondences alone do not replace estimates, and estimates alone do not provide the exact source comparison.

Hyperbolic orbicurve

The quotient of a once-punctured elliptic curve by its order-two involution is a basic orbicurve input. It is the geometric object behind many fundamental-group and cusp reconstructions.

Tempered fundamental group

Tempered fundamental groups encode covering data used in IUT I's anabelian reconstruction layer. They are distinct from the later mono-analytic comparison interfaces.

Decomposition subgroup

Decomposition subgroups recover local valuation and cusp information from group-theoretic data. They are part of the source machinery for local prime-strip objects.

Model base-NF bridge

The model base-NF bridge is the number-field side of the bridge formalism in IUT I. It transports capsule data while preserving the appropriate multiplicative labels.

Model base-Theta bridge

The model base-Theta bridge is the theta side of the same bridge discipline. It is tied to evaluation sections and label-class transport at bad places.

D-ThetaNF-Hodge theater

A D-ThetaNF-Hodge theater combines compatible D-NF and D-Theta bridges over a shared capsule of D-prime-strips.

Holomorphic procession

A procession organizes bridge and prime-strip data over label-indexed capsules. Later procession-normalized log-volumes inherit this label discipline.

ThetaNF-Hodge theater

A ThetaNF-Hodge theater glues theta and number-field bridge structures into a combined Hodge-theater interface. It prepares the later additive constructions.

Theta cyclotome

Definition 5.2 uses theta-side cyclotomes to compare Kummer classes of coric rational functions with roots of unity attached to the Frobenioid side.

Theta bridge

Theta bridges carry theta-side structure in the Hodge-theater taxonomy. They must be kept distinct from NF bridges and from theta links.

NF bridge

NF bridges carry number-field-side bridge structure. They participate in gluing operations but do not by themselves constitute theta evaluation.

Theta-plus-minus bridge

The additive theory introduces plus/minus theta bridges to manage the additive symmetry layer associated to labels and cusps.

Theta-ell bridge

Theta-ell bridges encode the elliptic/torsion side of the additive combinatorial Teichmuller theory in IUT I, Section 6.

Theta-plus-minus-ellNF theater

This theater combines additive theta-plus-minus, theta-ell, and NF data. It is the source object for the theta and log-theta constructions in IUT II and III.

Theta evaluation

Galois-theoretic theta evaluation reconstructs theta-value data from mono-theta and group-theoretic information. It is a source for the horizontal theta links.

Tempered Gaussian Frobenioids

Tempered Gaussian Frobenioids encode Gaussian theta-value distributions in a Frobenioid-theoretic setting before the global Gaussian layer.

Global Gaussian Frobenioids

Global Gaussian Frobenioids synchronize local Gaussian data and supply the realified Frobenioid objects used in theta-link comparisons.

Theta×μ link

The Theta×μ link is the full poly-isomorphism F×μ_env -> F×μ_△ of IUT II Corollary 4.10(iii). It is obtained by functorially upgrading a nonempty set of isomorphisms of F-prime-strips to isomorphisms of F×μ-prime-strips.

Theta×μ-gau link

The Gaussian version is the full poly-isomorphism F×μ_gau -> F×μ_△. Corollary 4.10 treats it parallel to the Theta×μ link, while Remark 4.10.2 explains why the later papers mainly use its enhanced descendants.

Coric F-prime-strip

Coric F⊢×μ-prime-strip data is invariant under both theta-link variants. Corollary 4.10(iv) identifies F⊢×μ_△ across theaters and then induces the corresponding D⊢_△ poly-isomorphism.

Multiradial theta monoids

IUT III Section 2 develops theta monoids whose constructions remain meaningful across the relevant alien holomorphic viewpoints.

Local holomorphic tensor packet

IUT III, Proposition 3.1 starts with an n-capsule of F-prime-strips. Over v_Q, the one-packet is the direct sum of the local logarithmic modules over all v | v_Q; the n-packet is their tensor product, equivalently a direct sum over all choices of one place in each tensor factor.

Local mono-analytic tensor packet

IUT III, Proposition 3.2 repeats the tensor-packet construction for D⊢-prime-strips, and when these arise from F⊢×μ-prime-strips it records the poly-isomorphisms to the holomorphic packets. The integral structures now come from mono-analytic log-shells.

Global tensor packet

IUT III, Proposition 3.3 defines (†M_mod)_A as a tensor product over α∈A of the labeled fields (†M_mod)_α for a subset of Hodge-theater labels and embeds it into the product of the local holomorphic tensor packets by localization composed with the given log-links.

Different estimates

IUT IV begins with estimates for differents in tensor products of local fields. These estimates feed the explicit log-volume bounds.

Normalized log-volume estimates

Nonarchimedean normalized log-volume estimates are the local numerical estimates that later combine into the theta-pilot log-volume theorem.

Prime number theorem input

IUT IV uses a prime-number-theorem estimate as part of the elementary analytic input for effective global inequalities.

Arithmetic divisors

Arithmetic divisors in IUT IV package the global arithmetic data whose degrees and heights enter the final Diophantine estimates.

Mutation history

A mutation history records a sequence of species-level transformations. It is the formal language for tracking inter-universal movement without collapsing implementations.

Anabelian Background

IUT is built on anabelian reconstruction: information about arithmetic curves is recovered from fundamental groups and related Galois-theoretic structures. Collas describes IUT as a continuation of Grothendieck's anabelian program through a new geometry of monoids; Mochizuki's gateway report frames IUT as a route from Diophantine geometry toward analytic number theory. Recent Collas papers also place this background in the active France-Japan anabelian and Galois-Teichmuller program.

Classical anabelian input

Hyperbolic curves, their coverings, and their arithmetic fundamental groups are the base objects. IUT I uses this input when reconstructing local decomposition data, cusps, and valuation-indexed structures.

Combinatorial and mono-anabelian methods

Hoshi and Mochizuki's background work turns reconstruction into functorial algorithms. The wiki treats these algorithms as a prerequisite layer for Hodge theaters and prime-strips.

Geometry of monoids

Collas emphasizes IUT's monoid geometry as a way to move beyond direct ring/scheme comparison. This is exactly why the wiki keeps separate pages for Frobenioids, log-shells, and prime-strip interfaces.

Fukugen reconstruction

Collas presents anabelian geometry as reconstruction, or fukugen: recover a space from its etale fundamental group and the functorial symmetries encoded by covers. The IUT relevance is that reconstruction is algorithmic input, not merely a philosophical analogy.

Collas §2.2reconstruction

Two-step monoid recovery

In Collas's summary of absolute mono-anabelian reconstruction, one first reconstructs multiplicative data through Kummer-theoretic containers, then reconstructs additive structure by using valuation quotients of function fields. This separates the two monoid structures before recovering a field.

Collas §2.2.2Kummer

Mono-anabelian transport

Collas describes mono-anabelian transport as moving Frobenius-like monoid data through etale-like containers, subject to indeterminacies. This is the expository bridge from p-adic anabelian reconstruction to IUT's theta-link and log-link transport.

Collas §2.2.3transport

Second regeometrization

Collas frames IUT as a second regeometrization of arithmetic geometry: after anabelian geometry unified arithmetic and geometry through fundamental groups, IUT tries to include Diophantine estimates by dismantling and comparing additive and multiplicative monoid structures.

Collas §3IUT framing

Hodge-theater synchronization

Collas explains Hodge theaters as synchronizing geometric symmetries of the pointed elliptic curve side with arithmetic symmetries of the number-field side. This is the expository version of the theater as a typed environment for the later theta/log link operations.

Gateway framing

Mochizuki's anabelian gateway report presents IUT as a bridge from Diophantine geometry to analytic number theory. The practical link inside the main series is through log-volume estimates.

N-th power monoid map

The Gateway report uses the N-th power map as an elementary model: it is an isomorphism of multiplicative monoids but not a ring homomorphism. This is the basic reason the common multiplicative interface cannot be read as equality of ring structures.

Gateway §2monoid not ring

Projective-line gluing analogy

The same report compares IUT gluing to constructing P1 by gluing two labeled affine lines along Gm using inversion. Deleting the labels turns a meaningful gluing into a contradiction unless one passes to a quotient or collection of possibilities.

Gateway §3.1labels

ZFCG and data types

Gateway §3.3 stresses that IUT is formulated in ZFC with Grothendieck universes and works with diagrams and species-like packages of data, not only with selected underlying sets. This supports the wiki's insistence on typed records and closed-loop comparisons.

Gateway §3.3species

Galois-orbit IUT

Section 4 describes a Galois-orbit version of IUT as work in progress, with claimed applications to local-global section-conjecture conditions, Siegel zeroes for imaginary-quadratic Dirichlet L-functions, and stronger abc/Szpiro inequalities.

Formalization implication

Lean development should not treat anabelian reconstruction as a black box forever. It can start as typed interfaces, but the long-term project must replace those interfaces by constructions from anabelian source results.

Prime Strips and Bridges

Prime-strips are valuation-indexed interfaces extracted from Hodge-theater data. They are not decorative labels: D-prime-strips, their mono-analytic versions, F-prime-strips, and bridge data determine which information is retained when the theory moves between holomorphic and mono-analytic views.

Prime-strip interface holomorphic data, mono-analyticization, and bridges

D-prime-strip

IUT I Definition 4.1 defines a D-prime-strip as a collection {D_v}_{v in V}. At nonarchimedean places these are categories equivalent to the local D-data; at archimedean places they are Aut-holomorphic orbispaces.

Mono-analyticization

The associated D⊢-prime-strip keeps the mono-analytic information obtained by passing to the appropriate subcategories or topological-monoid objects. This is one controlled way to weaken holomorphic history.

Label classes

Label classes of cusps are reconstructed from the prime-strip and carry an F_l-torsor structure together with a canonical class determined by the initial cusp datum.

D-NF bridge

A D-NF bridge is a poly-morphism from a capsule of D-prime-strips to a number-field side D-prime-strip. It tracks the number-field component of a bridge without conflating it with theta evaluation.

D-Theta bridge

A D-Theta bridge is a poly-morphism from a capsule of D-prime-strips to a theta-side D-prime-strip. At bad places it transports the label information used later by theta-value and Gaussian constructions.

D-ThetaNF theater

A D-ThetaNF-Hodge theater combines compatible D-NF and D-Theta bridges over the same capsule. This compatibility is an early instance of the "same interface, different permitted histories" discipline.

Formalization checkpoint: a Lean model should distinguish the strip object, the mono-analyticized strip, the capsule index set, and the two bridge directions. Collapsing these loses exactly the bookkeeping needed later in the log-theta lattice.

Species, Mutations, and Inter-universal Formalism

IUT IV Section 3 introduces species to make explicit a kind of mathematical bookkeeping that is often left informal: a "type of object" is described by set-theoretic formulas, interpreted in ZFC-models, rather than by one preferred set of representatives. Mutations are formula-defined transformations between species. Mutation histories, observables, and cores then describe what changes and what remains invariant when the theory moves through distinct copies.

Species-level movement rules, specimens, mutations, and cores

0-species and 1-species

Definition 3.1 starts with a 0-species: a set-theoretic formula for object data. A 1-species adds formula-defined morphism data, unique composites, associativity, and identities. A species is the pair consisting of these object and morphism formulas.

Definition 3.1(i)-(iii)formulas

Formula absoluteness

The formulas used in §3 are required to be absolute under universe extension: if the relevant data lies in a universe, interpreting the same formula in a larger universe gives the same validity. This is the technical discipline behind model-free specimens.

Definition 3.1 setupuniverses

Species

A species is a pair consisting of a 0-species of objects and a 1-species of morphisms acting on them. Each side is given by formulas whose specimens are realized inside a chosen ZFC-model.

Definition 3.1objects/morphisms

Model-free specimens

A model-free specimen is described by a formula that gives the same specimen when interpreted after passing to a larger universe. This is the formal meaning of "inter-universal" in IUT IV Section 3.

Remarks 3.1.2-3.1.4ZFCG

Mutations

A mutation is a formula-defined transformation from one species to another, compatible with identities and composites. In a fixed ZFC-model it behaves like a functor between the corresponding categories.

Definition 3.3(i)functorial

Morphisms of mutations

A morphism of mutations is a formula that assigns, to each source object, a morphism between the two mutated outputs, compatibly with every source morphism. In a fixed ZFC-model it is the species-theoretic analogue of a natural transformation.

Definition 3.3(ii)natural transformation

Mutation equivalence

A mutation-equivalence has a mutation quasi-inverse up to isomorphism of mutations. Mochizuki uses this to express reconstruction-style equivalence without choosing one privileged concrete representative.

Definition 3.3(iii)quasi-inverse

Mutation histories

A mutation history is an oriented graph whose vertices carry species and whose edges carry mutations. This is the species-theoretic analogue of a diagram of categories.

Definition 3.3(iv)graph

Observables and cores

An observable reads a mutation history into a fixed target species. A core is a covariant observable whose transition morphisms are isomorphisms, so it records an invariant portion of the history.

Definition 3.3(v)coricity

Essential Logical Structure

Mochizuki's 2024 logical-structure report presents IUT as a chain of simultaneous theta-link relations together with controlled enlargements by indeterminacies. The source slogan is not that all copies become one copy, but that one maintains an AND relation while replacing one side by larger collections of possibilities.

Boolean skeleton theta-link AND plus indeterminacy XOR

AND/XOR chain

Section 3.10 summarizes the proof architecture as a chain A ∧ (B1 ⊻ B2 ...) enlarged by later possibility terms. The AND terms correspond to simultaneous theta-link execution; the XOR terms correspond to alternatives produced by indeterminacy and projection/subquotient operations.

§3.10∧/⊻

Redundant-copy warning

The report's central warning is that arbitrarily identifying copies of Frobenius-like, etale-like, log-linked, or theta-linked objects changes the logical form of IUT. Such identifications can make coricity look trivial while erasing the labels that make the comparison meaningful.

§3.1-3.4labels

Boolean carry analogy

Mochizuki compares the AND/XOR pattern to carry addition for truncated Witt vectors: multiplication-like common structure and addition-like non-common structure are intertwined. The analogy is explanatory; the wiki treats it as a guide to the logical shape, not as a substitute for the source constructions.

LANA

LANA stands for Lean for ANAbelian geometry. ZEN Mathematics Center announced it publicly on 31 March 2026 as an international project involving ZEN University, Utrecht University, and the University of Alberta. Its first aim is Lean formalization of anabelian geometry; its second aim is formal verification of IUT.

Core members reported by ZEN

Fumiharu Kato, Johan Commelin, Kiran Kedlaya, Yuichiro Hoshi, and Adam Topaz, with additional junior participants.

Status as of public announcement

ZEN reports work since autumn 2023, a neutral stance, substantial clarity about understood and unresolved points, and an interim progress report planned for 17 July 2026.

Anabelian library first

The public remarks emphasize formalizing principal theorems of anabelian geometry and building a Lean library, including foundations such as Galois categories and fundamental groups, before using that base for IUT.

Unresolved point policy

The LANA remarks state that the team has repeatedly encountered a specific IUT point, can articulate it with enough precision to target Lean, but has not concluded whether the remaining obstacle is a mathematical gap or a limit of current understanding.

Neutral verification stance

Kato's public remarks describe LANA as aiming neither to defend nor reject IUT in advance. The stated task is to formalize enough of the theory to make the unresolved points precise and then continue the dialogue with Mochizuki.

2026 announcementneutral

Human understanding first

The announcement stresses that IUT cannot be treated as a mechanical translation task. The team reports bootcamps in Japan, Canada, the Netherlands, and the United States, aimed at building shared mathematical understanding before and during formalization.

bootcampsIUT study

Lean is not generative AI

Commelin's remarks distinguish Lean verification from generative AI prediction: Lean checks formal proofs against logical rules, while humans remain responsible for choosing definitions and ensuring that statements match the intended mathematics.

Topaz and Hoshi both describe a repeatedly encountered point in the logic around Theorem 3.11 to Corollary 3.12. Publicly, LANA has not classified this point as either a proof gap or a limitation of present understanding.

Anabelian geometry
Reconstruction of arithmetic/geometric objects from fundamental-group-like data; a major prerequisite background for IUT.

Algorithmic parallel transport through IUT gluings, up to specified indeterminacy.

The proof-critical IUT III comparison that supplies the signed log-volume inequality used downstream.

Categorical packaging of arithmetic line-bundle/divisor/monoid data used in Mochizuki's earlier infrastructure.

A miniature arithmetic world associated to initial theta data, designed to separate additive and multiplicative structures.

Controlled ambiguity allowed by the IUT comparison; in the key corridor, Ind3 is one-sided upper-semi rather than equality.

Input prime-strip link property tying output possibilities to input prime-strip data at the comparison boundary.
Kummer detachment
The IUT III passage from Frobenius-like objects in the Theta×μ-LGP link to etale-like objects in the multiradial representation, using the full log-Kummer correspondence.

Lean for ANAbelian geometry, a ZEN Mathematics Center project to formalize anabelian geometry and verify IUT.

Vertical link in the log-theta lattice, formed via local logarithms and log-shells.

Local logarithmic object associated to units; used to manage additive log data.

Two-dimensional diagram of Hodge theaters whose horizontal arrows are theta links and vertical arrows are log links.

Meaningful from multiple radial or alien holomorphic viewpoints, rather than tied to one ordinary scheme history.
